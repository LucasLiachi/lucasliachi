# Summary — ITIL 4 Framework

## What is ITIL 4

ITIL 4 (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) is a best-practice framework for **IT service management (ITSM)**. Version 4, launched in 2019, integrates concepts from Agile, DevOps, and Lean into the traditional process model.

**Core objective:** create, deliver, and continually improve IT services that generate value for customers and stakeholders.

## Service Value System (SVS)

The SVS is the core of ITIL 4. It transforms demand and opportunity into value through:

```
Opportunity / Demand
        ↓
   [Value Chain]  ←→  [ITIL Practices]
        ↓
     VALUE
       ↑
  (guided by the 7 Guiding Principles)
  (governed by the Governance Model)
  (improved by Continual Improvement)
```

## The 7 Guiding Principles

| Principle | Practical summary |
|---|---|
| **Focus on value** | Every action must generate value for the customer or stakeholders |
| **Start where you are** | Assess the current state before creating something new |
| **Progress iteratively with feedback** | Deliver value in increments, adjust with learning |
| **Collaborate and promote visibility** | Work together, share information, avoid silos |
| **Think and work holistically** | Consider the entire system, not just isolated parts |
| **Keep it simple and practical** | Eliminate steps that add no value |
| **Optimize and automate** | Use human resources for what cannot be automated |

## Service Value Chain (SVC) — 6 activities

| Activity | Role |
|---|---|
| **Plan** | Ensure shared understanding and direction of vision, improvements and plans |
| **Improve** | Continual improvement of practices, services and products |
| **Engage** | Good understanding of stakeholder needs |
| **Design & Transition** | Services and practices meeting quality expectations |
| **Obtain/Build** | Provisioning of service components |
| **Deliver & Support** | Delivery and support of services as specified |

## Key Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Service** | Means of co-creating value, facilitating outcomes without the customer managing costs and risks |
| **Value** | Perception of benefits, usefulness and importance |
| **Utility** | Functionality offered ("what it does") — fit for purpose |
| **Warranty** | Confirmation that the service meets requirements — fit for use |
| **Outcome** | Result for the customer/stakeholder enabled by the service |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement — between provider and customer |
| **OLA** | Operational Level Agreement — internal, between teams |
| **UC** | Underpinning Contract — with external supplier |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed — roles model |

## Differences ITIL v3 vs ITIL 4

| Aspect | ITIL v3 | ITIL 4 |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | 5 books (lifecycle) | SVS + Value Chain |
| Terminology | Processes | Practices |
| Integration | Focused on ITSM | Includes Agile, DevOps, Lean |
| Quantity | 26 processes | 34 practices |
| Focus | Operations and efficiency | Value and co-creation |
